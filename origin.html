Origin of the E-mail


Shiva Ayyadurai
Ray Tomlinson


Ray Tomlinson is credited as the inventor of networked email; in 1971, he developed the first system able to send mail between users on different hosts across the ARPANET, using the @ sign to link the user name with a destination server. By the mid-1970s, this was the form recognized as email. Meanwhile in 1978, a 14-year-old boy, Shiva Ayyadurai began his work on an email system for the University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey. Queen Elizabeth becomes the first head of state to use ARPANET's electronic mail
For a time in the late 1980s and early 1990s, it seemed likely that either a proprietary commercial system or the X. 400 email system, part of the Government Open Systems Interconnection Profile (GOSIP), would predominate. However, once the final restrictions on carrying commercial traffic over the Internet ended in 1995, a combination of factors made the current Internet suite of SMTP, POP3 and IMAP email protocols the standard.
Microsoft releases its first commercial email client, Microsoft Mail,a precursor to Outlook in 1988
Blackberry introduces a mobile phone with wireless email support in 2002.
